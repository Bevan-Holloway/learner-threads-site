Why Nurture Dispositions?

Being a good learner has nothing to do with IQ. [cite: 22] It means engaging intelligently with things that are complicated, confusing, and challenging in order to grow ever more competent. [cite: 23] Nurturing a core set of dispositions, such as curiosity and perseverance, allows all learners to stretch their intelligence beyond what they thought possible. [cite: 24, 25]

üîç Curiosity: The Engine of Learning [cite: 36]

Curious Learners...

  • Have a questioning and positive attitude to learning. [cite: 124]
  • Like exploring new things and meeting new people. [cite: 126]
  • Relish being amazed. [cite: 127]
  • Enjoy researching and use the skills needed to do so effectively. [cite: 128]

To Stretch Curiosity, Try...

  • Asking questions like "What does that make you wonder?" [cite: 132] or "What's missing here?". [cite: 140]
  • Using strategies like provocations, inquiry learning, and journaling. [cite: 142, 144, 146]
  • Fostering a culture that values playfulness and taking risks. [cite: 149, 150]

üí™ Persistence: Staying Engaged [cite: 40]

Persistent Learners...

  • Are willing to "have a go" and take risks. [cite: 163]
  • Don't panic when they don't immediately have an answer. [cite: 164, 165, 166]
  • Put in the time to practise hard skills. [cite: 168]
  • Know when to take a break and return refreshed. [cite: 171]

To Stretch Persistence, Try...

  • Asking questions like "What resources could help you?" [cite: 180, 181] or "Is there a smaller step here?". [cite: 178]
  • Using strategies like puzzles, riddles, and learning stories. [cite: 184, 185]
  • Fostering a culture that values seeing things through and psychological safety. [cite: 187, 189]
